Submission Tips

Eligibility Criteria

To apply for grants through KEMRI, researchers must meet the following criteria:

  • Be an employed staff member of KEMRI or affiliated with a KEMRI research center
  • Hold a minimum qualification of a Master's degree in a relevant health or biomedical science field
  • Have a research proposal that aligns with KEMRI's research priorities and ethical guidelines
  • Complete KEMRI's mandatory research ethics training program
  • Have no outstanding reports or financial issues from previous grants
  • Meet any additional specific requirements set by the funding agency

Note: Early-career researchers may apply under the mentorship of a senior researcher who meets all the above criteria.

Submission Tips

Before Submission

  • Start early – at least 4-6 weeks before the deadline
  • Read the funding announcement carefully, multiple times
  • Ensure your research question clearly addresses the funder's priorities
  • Get feedback from colleagues and the GMD team
  • Proofread thoroughly to eliminate typos and grammatical errors
  • Ensure all required documents are prepared according to specified formats

During Submission

  • Submit at least 24-48 hours before the deadline to avoid last-minute technical issues
  • Ensure all attachments are in the correct format and within size limits
  • Keep a record of your submission confirmation
  • Have a backup plan in case of system failures or internet issues
  • Have the GMD team review your final submission package

After Submission

  • Follow up to confirm receipt of your application
  • Respond promptly to any requests for clarification or additional information
  • Begin preparing for the implementation phase
  • If unsuccessful, request feedback to improve future applications
  • Inform the GMD team of the submission outcome

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the internal review process take?

The internal review process typically takes 2-3 weeks, depending on the complexity of the proposal and the workload of the review committee. We recommend submitting your proposal for internal review at least one month before the funding agency's deadline.

Can I apply for multiple grants simultaneously?

Yes, researchers can apply for multiple grants simultaneously, but it's important to ensure that you have the capacity to implement all potential projects if multiple applications are successful. The GMD team can help you assess your capacity and develop a manageable grant portfolio.

Do I need to inform GMD about grants I'm applying for independently?

Yes, all grant applications that will be implemented through KEMRI or use KEMRI's name and resources must be registered with the GMD, even if you are developing the application independently. This is to ensure institutional oversight and avoid potential conflicts of interest or resource constraints.

How much time should I allocate for proposal development?

For complex, multi-year projects, we recommend allocating 2-3 months for proposal development. For smaller grants, 4-6 weeks is usually sufficient. Starting early gives you time for thorough literature review, methodological refinement, budget calculations, internal reviews, and revisions.

What support does GMD provide after a grant is awarded?

After a grant is awarded, GMD provides support for contract negotiation, project setup, financial management, compliance monitoring, progress reporting, stakeholder communication, and project closure. Our goal is to ensure smooth project implementation and compliance with both funder requirements and institutional policies.
