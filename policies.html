Compliance Requirements

All research projects conducted at KEMRI must adhere to specific compliance requirements, including:

  • Ethical Approval: Research involving human subjects must receive approval from KEMRI's Ethical Review Committee
  • Financial Compliance: Adherence to institutional and funder financial regulations
  • Reporting: Timely submission of technical and financial reports as per the grant agreement
  • Data Management: Proper collection, storage, and sharing of research data as per established protocols
  • Publication Policy: Compliance with open access requirements and proper acknowledgment of funding sources
  • Environmental, Health, and Safety: Adherence to safety guidelines in laboratory and field research

For detailed guidance on compliance requirements, please consult the specific policy documents or contact the Grant Management Department.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often are policy documents updated?

KEMRI policy documents are reviewed and updated on a biennial basis, or more frequently if significant changes in regulations or best practices occur. The date of the last update is indicated on each policy document.

Are there different policies for different types of grants?

While the core policies apply to all grants, certain funding agencies may have specific additional requirements. The GMD team provides guidance on agency-specific requirements for each grant.

How can I get clarification on policy interpretation?

For questions related to policy interpretation, please contact the GMD Policy Officer at policy.gmd@kemri.org or schedule a consultation through our contact page.

What happens if I don't comply with a policy requirement?

Non-compliance with institutional policies may result in project delays, funding holds, or in severe cases, termination of the grant. Early communication with the GMD team about compliance challenges can help identify appropriate solutions.
