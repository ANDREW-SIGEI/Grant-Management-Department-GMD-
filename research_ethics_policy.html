Research Ethics Policy

KEMRI's ethical guidelines for conducting medical and health research

KEMRI GRANT MANAGEMENT DEPARTMENT

RESEARCH ETHICS POLICY

1. INTRODUCTION

  • Purpose of the policy: This policy provides guidance on ethical considerations in the design, approval, and conduct of research at KEMRI, ensuring that all research activities adhere to the highest ethical standards.
  • Scope of application: This policy applies to all research conducted by or affiliated with KEMRI, including collaborative research with other institutions.
  • Definitions of key terms: Clarification of important terminology used throughout the policy for consistent interpretation and application.

2. ETHICAL PRINCIPLES

  • Respect for persons: Recognition of the autonomy of individuals and special protections for those with diminished autonomy.
  • Beneficence: Obligation to maximize potential benefits and minimize possible harms.
  • Justice: Fair distribution of the benefits and burdens of research.
  • Scientific integrity: Commitment to rigorous research methods and accurate reporting of findings.
  • Responsible stewardship: Careful management of research resources, including data and biological materials.

3. RESEARCH ETHICS REVIEW PROCESS

  • Types of research requiring ethics review: Definition of research activities that require formal ethics review.
  • Composition of ethics review committee: Structure and membership requirements for ethics committees.
  • Submission requirements: Documentation and information required for ethics review.
  • Review procedures: Process for initial and continuing review of research protocols.
  • Decision-making process: Criteria and procedures for approving, requiring modifications to, or disapproving research.
  • Appeals process: Procedures for investigators to appeal committee decisions.

4. INFORMED CONSENT

  • Elements of informed consent: Required information to be disclosed to potential research participants.
  • Process of obtaining informed consent: Procedures for ensuring voluntary and informed decision-making.
  • Special considerations for vulnerable populations: Additional protections for children, pregnant women, prisoners, and persons with cognitive impairments.
  • Documentation requirements: Standards for documenting informed consent.
  • Waiver of informed consent: Criteria and procedures for waiving or altering informed consent requirements.

5. PRIVACY AND CONFIDENTIALITY

  • Data protection: Measures to safeguard personal and sensitive information.
  • Anonymization and pseudonymization: Procedures for protecting participant identities in research data.
  • Data sharing: Guidelines for sharing research data while protecting privacy.
  • Data security: Requirements for secure storage and transmission of research data.

6. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

  • Research involving vulnerable populations: Additional ethical considerations and safeguards.
  • Research in emergency situations: Ethical guidelines for research during emergencies.
  • Use of biological materials: Requirements for collection, storage, and use of human biological specimens.
  • Genetic research: Specific guidelines for research involving genetic information.
  • Community-based research: Considerations for research conducted within communities.

7. REPORTING ADVERSE EVENTS

  • Definition of adverse events: Classification of events requiring reporting.
  • Reporting procedures: Process for timely reporting of adverse events.
  • Investigation process: Procedures for investigating reported adverse events.
  • Corrective actions: Guidelines for addressing and preventing adverse events.

8. MONITORING AND AUDIT

  • Continuing review: Requirements for ongoing ethics review of approved research.
  • Site visits: Procedures for on-site monitoring of research activities.
  • Auditing process: Guidelines for systematic review of research procedures and documentation.
  • Non-compliance procedures: Process for addressing failure to comply with ethical requirements.

9. RESEARCH MISCONDUCT

  • Definition of research misconduct: Behaviors constituting research misconduct, including fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism.
  • Reporting procedures: Process for reporting suspected research misconduct.
  • Investigation process: Guidelines for investigating allegations of misconduct.
  • Sanctions: Potential consequences for confirmed research misconduct.

10. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

  • Definition of conflicts of interest: Situations constituting conflicts in research.
  • Disclosure requirements: Procedures for disclosing potential conflicts.
  • Management strategies: Approaches to managing identified conflicts of interest.

11. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

  • Required training for researchers: Ethics training expectations for research personnel.
  • Frequency of training: Guidelines for initial and refresher training.
  • Documentation of training: Requirements for documenting completion of ethics training.

12. POLICY IMPLEMENTATION

  • Roles and responsibilities: Assignment of responsibilities for implementing the policy.
  • Effective date: Date from which the policy takes effect.
  • Review and revision process: Procedures for periodic review and updating of the policy.

APPENDICES

  • Flowcharts
  • Checklists
  • References

This policy is effective from January 1, 2023 and will be reviewed every two years.
