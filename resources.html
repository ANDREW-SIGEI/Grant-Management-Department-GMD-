Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the grant application and management process

The first step is to develop a concept note that outlines your research idea, objectives, methodology, and potential impact. You can use our Concept Note Template to get started. Once your concept note is ready, submit it to the Grant Management Department for initial review and feedback before developing a full proposal.

The ethics review process typically takes 4-6 weeks from submission to approval, depending on the complexity of the research and whether revisions are required. We recommend building this timeframe into your project planning. Please review our Research Ethics Policy for detailed information about the review process.

Our department provides comprehensive support for budget preparation, including:

  • Budget templates and guidelines
  • Information on current salary scales and approved rates
  • Guidance on allowable costs for different funders
  • Review of draft budgets before submission
  • Calculation of indirect costs and institutional overheads

Contact the Grants Management Unit to schedule a budget consultation.

KEMRI's Partnerships & Resource Mobilisation Unit can help match you with potential collaborators based on your research focus. We maintain relationships with numerous local and international research institutions, universities, and funding agencies. You can also browse our Researchers page to see ongoing projects and research interests of KEMRI scientists.

Reporting requirements vary by funder, but typically include:

  • Technical/scientific progress reports (quarterly or semi-annual)
  • Financial reports (quarterly or semi-annual)
  • Annual reports
  • Final technical and financial reports
  • Publications and research outputs

Once your project is funded, the Grant Management Department will provide a detailed reporting schedule specific to your grant. You can use our Progress Report Template as a starting point for technical reporting.

To request a budget modification:

  1. Prepare a written justification explaining the requested changes and why they're necessary
  2. Prepare a comparison of the original and proposed revised budget
  3. Submit both documents to your assigned Grants Manager
  4. The Grant Management Department will review your request and advise on funder-specific requirements
  5. For significant modifications, funder approval may be required before implementation

Note that some funders have specific restrictions on budget modifications, and approval processes can take 2-4 weeks.
