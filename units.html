Department Units

Our specialized units providing comprehensive grant support at KEMRI

The Grant Management Department operates through three specialized units, each focused on different aspects of the grant lifecycle to ensure comprehensive support for KEMRI researchers and partners.

Pre-Award, Contracts & Compliance Unit

The Pre-Award, Contracts & Compliance Unit supports researchers during the crucial proposal development phase and ensures all funding applications and agreements align with institutional policies, sponsor requirements, and applicable regulations.

Key Functions

  • Proposal Development Support

    Assists researchers in developing competitive research proposals, including budget preparation, compliance review, and ensuring adherence to funder guidelines.

  • Opportunity Identification

    Identifies and disseminates information about funding opportunities from various sources to KEMRI researchers.

  • Contract Review & Negotiation

    Reviews, negotiates, and finalizes research contracts, collaborative agreements, and memoranda of understanding with funding agencies and collaborating institutions.

  • Compliance Management

    Ensures proposals and contracts comply with KEMRI policies, national regulations, and international standards for research ethics and integrity.

Deputy Director Grants Management

The Grants Management Unit oversees the post-award administration of research grants, ensuring proper financial management, reporting, and compliance throughout the project lifecycle.

Key Functions

  • Financial Management

    Monitors grant expenditures, ensures adherence to approved budgets, and provides financial reports to both researchers and funding agencies.

  • Technical & Financial Reporting

    Coordinates and supports the preparation and submission of technical and financial reports required by funding agencies.

  • Grant Closeout

    Manages the process of closing completed grants, ensuring all technical and financial requirements have been fulfilled.

  • Monitoring & Evaluation

    Tracks key performance indicators for research projects and facilitates periodic evaluations to ensure progress toward objectives.

Partnerships & Resource Mobilisation

The Partnerships & Resource Mobilisation Unit focuses on establishing and maintaining strategic relationships with funding agencies, research institutions, and other stakeholders to enhance KEMRI's research capacities and funding opportunities.

Key Functions

  • Strategic Partnerships

    Identifies, establishes, and manages partnerships with national and international research institutions, funding agencies, and other stakeholders.

  • Resource Mobilisation

    Develops and implements strategies to secure sustainable funding sources for KEMRI's research activities.

  • Capacity Building

    Coordinates training programs and workshops to enhance the capacity of KEMRI researchers in grant writing, project management, and other relevant skills.

  • Stakeholder Engagement

    Maintains regular communication with stakeholders, including government agencies, donors, and research partners, to ensure alignment with national health priorities and donor interests.

How Our Units Work Together

The three units of the Grant Management Department work collaboratively throughout the grant lifecycle to provide seamless support to KEMRI researchers:

1

Opportunity Identification & Planning

The Partnerships Unit identifies funding opportunities and potential collaborators, while the Pre-Award Unit helps researchers evaluate the fit with their research interests.

2

Proposal Development

The Pre-Award Unit provides comprehensive support for proposal development, with input from the Grants Management Unit on budgeting and resource allocation.

3

Award Negotiation & Setup

The Pre-Award Unit leads contract negotiations, while the Grants Management Unit prepares for project implementation and financial tracking.

4

Project Implementation

The Grants Management Unit oversees project execution, with the Pre-Award Unit handling any contract modifications and the Partnerships Unit managing stakeholder relationships.

5

Project Closeout & Sustainability

The Grants Management Unit manages the closeout process, while the Partnerships Unit explores opportunities for continued funding or new collaborations.
