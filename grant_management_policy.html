Grant Management Policy

Comprehensive policy outlining the management of grants at KEMRI

KEMRI GRANT MANAGEMENT DEPARTMENT

GRANT MANAGEMENT POLICY

1. INTRODUCTION

  • Purpose of the policy: This policy establishes guidelines and procedures for the effective and compliant management of research grants at KEMRI, ensuring that all grant-related activities are conducted with integrity, transparency, and in accordance with funder requirements.
  • Scope and applicability: This policy applies to all KEMRI staff, researchers, and administrators involved in any aspect of the grant lifecycle, from pre-award to post-award activities.
  • Definitions of key terms: Includes explanations of important terminology used throughout the policy document for clarity and consistency.

2. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Director of Scientific Programmes, Partnerships and Grants Management: Oversees all grant management activities and ensures alignment with KEMRI's strategic objectives.
  • Pre-Award, Contracts & Compliance Unit: Responsible for proposal development, submission, and compliance reviews.
  • Deputy Director Grants Management: Oversees financial administration, procurement, and human resources for grants.
  • Partnerships & Resource Mobilisation: Facilitates collaborations and identifies funding opportunities.
  • Principal Investigators: Lead the scientific aspects of research projects and manage project implementation.
  • Researchers: Conduct research activities according to protocols and funding requirements.
  • Administrative staff: Provide support for grant-related administrative functions.

3. PRE-AWARD PROCEDURES

  • Identifying funding opportunities: Processes for identifying and disseminating appropriate funding opportunities.
  • Proposal development: Guidelines for developing competitive research proposals.
  • Budget preparation: Standards for preparing accurate and compliant project budgets.
  • Internal review process: Procedures for internal review before submission.
  • Institutional approvals: Required approvals from KEMRI leadership and committees.
  • Submission procedures: Processes for timely and accurate submission of proposals.
  • Grant negotiation: Guidelines for negotiating grant terms and conditions.

4. POST-AWARD PROCEDURES

  • Award acceptance: Processes for reviewing and accepting grant awards.
  • Project setup: Procedures for establishing project accounts and structures.
  • Budget management: Guidelines for managing project budgets effectively.
  • Procurement procedures: Standards for procurement of goods and services under grants.
  • Human resource management: Processes for recruiting and managing project staff.
  • Financial reporting: Requirements for accurate and timely financial reporting.
  • Technical reporting: Guidelines for preparing and submitting technical progress reports.
  • No-cost extensions: Procedures for requesting and implementing no-cost extensions.
  • Budget modifications: Processes for requesting and implementing budget modifications.

5. FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

  • Cost principles: Overarching principles guiding financial management of grants.
  • Allowable costs: Guidelines for determining allowable grant expenditures.
  • Unallowable costs: Categories of costs that cannot be charged to grants.
  • Cost sharing: Procedures for managing and documenting cost sharing commitments.
  • Indirect costs: Guidelines for applying and managing indirect cost recovery.
  • Financial documentation: Requirements for financial record-keeping.
  • Payment procedures: Processes for requesting and processing grant-related payments.
  • Financial monitoring: Methods for ongoing monitoring of grant finances.

6. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

  • Regulatory compliance: Requirements for adhering to relevant regulations.
  • Ethical requirements: Guidelines for ethical conduct of research.
  • Intellectual property: Policies regarding intellectual property arising from grant-funded research.
  • Data management: Requirements for proper management of research data.
  • Publication policy: Guidelines for publications resulting from grant-funded research.
  • Conflict of interest: Procedures for identifying and managing conflicts of interest.

7. MONITORING AND EVALUATION

  • Performance monitoring: Methods for tracking project implementation against objectives.
  • Progress reporting: Requirements for periodic progress reporting.
  • Site visits: Procedures for conducting and responding to site visits.
  • Audit procedures: Guidelines for external and internal audits of grant activities.
  • Non-compliance management: Processes for addressing instances of non-compliance.

8. GRANT CLOSEOUT

  • Technical completion: Requirements for completing and documenting technical aspects.
  • Financial closeout: Procedures for closing financial accounts and reporting.
  • Record retention: Requirements for retaining grant-related records.
  • Equipment disposition: Guidelines for disposition of equipment purchased with grant funds.
  • Intellectual property management: Procedures for managing intellectual property after grant completion.

9. PARTNERSHIPS AND COLLABORATIONS

  • Types of partnerships: Descriptions of different partnership models supported by KEMRI.
  • Memoranda of Understanding: Guidelines for establishing formal partnership agreements.
  • Collaborative agreements: Requirements for agreements with research collaborators.
  • Sub-awards management: Procedures for establishing and managing sub-awards.
  • International collaborations: Special considerations for international partnerships.

10. CAPACITY BUILDING

  • Training programs: Description of training opportunities for grant management skills.
  • Professional development: Support for ongoing professional development.
  • Technical assistance: Availability of technical assistance for researchers.
  • Resource development: Ongoing development of grant management resources.

11. POLICY IMPLEMENTATION

  • Effective date: Date from which the policy takes effect.
  • Review cycle: Frequency and procedures for policy review and updates.
  • Amendment procedures: Processes for amending the policy.
  • Related policies and procedures: List of related KEMRI policies and procedures.

APPENDICES

  • Templates and forms
  • Checklists
  • Process flowcharts
  • Contact information

This policy is effective from January 1, 2023 and will be reviewed every two years.
