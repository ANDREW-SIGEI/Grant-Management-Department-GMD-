Ongoing Projects

Explore the diverse range of research projects currently supported by our department.

Malaria Vaccine Efficacy Study in Endemic Regions of Kenya

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Jane Muthoni
Funder: Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
Duration: 2021-2024 (3 years)
Research Area: Infectious Diseases, Vaccine Development

A phase III clinical trial evaluating the efficacy and safety of a novel malaria vaccine candidate in children aged 6 months to 5 years in high-transmission areas of Western Kenya.

Partners: University of Oxford, CDC, Ministry of Health

Multi-Drug Resistant Tuberculosis Surveillance and Management

Active
Principal Investigator: Prof. John Kariuki
Funder: National Institutes of Health (NIH)
Duration: 2022-2026 (4 years)
Research Area: Infectious Diseases, Antimicrobial Resistance

A comprehensive study of multi-drug resistant tuberculosis patterns across Kenya, focusing on developing improved diagnostic tools and treatment protocols for affected populations.

Partners: Johns Hopkins University, National TB Program

Impact of Climate Change on Vector-Borne Diseases in East Africa

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Sarah Khamis
Funder: Wellcome Trust
Duration: 2021-2025 (4 years)
Research Area: Public Health, Environmental Health

An interdisciplinary research project investigating the relationship between climate change variables and the prevalence and distribution of vector-borne diseases across Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda.

Partners: London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, University of Nairobi

Maternal Nutrition and Child Development Study

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Elizabeth Wanjiru
Funder: USAID
Duration: 2022-2025 (3 years)
Research Area: Maternal & Child Health, Nutrition

A longitudinal study examining the impact of maternal nutrition interventions during pregnancy on child cognitive and physical development outcomes in rural Kenyan communities.

Partners: Aga Khan University, Ministry of Health

Digital Health Solutions for Community Health Workers

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Michael Ochieng
Funder: World Health Organization
Duration: 2023-2025 (2 years)
Research Area: Health Systems, Digital Health

Implementation research evaluating the effectiveness of mobile health applications in improving the performance and data collection capabilities of community health workers across diverse Kenyan counties.

Partners: Safaricom, Ministry of Health, University of Washington

HIV Prevention Among Adolescent Girls and Young Women

Active
Principal Investigator: Prof. Grace Kemunto
Funder: EDCTP
Duration: 2021-2024 (3 years)
Research Area: Infectious Diseases, Sexual & Reproductive Health

A community-based intervention study testing innovative approaches to HIV prevention among adolescent girls and young women in high-prevalence regions of Kenya.

Partners: UNAIDS, NASCOP, University of Manitoba

Genomic Surveillance of Emerging Infectious Diseases

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Samuel Mwangi
Funder: Wellcome Trust
Duration: 2022-2027 (5 years)
Research Area: Infectious Diseases, Genomics

Establishment of genomic surveillance network for early detection and monitoring of emerging infectious diseases across Kenya, with a focus on zoonotic pathogens with pandemic potential.

Partners: Africa CDC, Wellcome Sanger Institute, University of Cambridge

Non-Communicable Disease Risk Factors in Urban Populations

Active
Principal Investigator: Dr. Faith Njoki
Funder: National Institutes of Health (NIH)
Duration: 2023-2026 (3 years)
Research Area: Non-Communicable Diseases, Urban Health

A comprehensive study of non-communicable disease risk factors among urban populations in Kenya, with a focus on hypertension, diabetes, and obesity in informal settlements.

Partners: Kenyatta National Hospital, APHRC, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health

Research Impact

Our funded research projects have made significant contributions to global health knowledge and local health outcomes:

  • Development of new diagnostic tools for infectious diseases
  • Implementation of effective community-based health interventions
  • Training of over 200 early-career researchers
  • Publication of 150+ peer-reviewed articles in the past five years
  • Influence on national health policies and guidelines
  • Improved health service delivery in underserved communities

For more information on specific project impacts, please contact the Grant Management Department or the principal investigators directly.
